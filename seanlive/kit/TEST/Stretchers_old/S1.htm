Log Roll

 

Why we Log Roll patients:-

 

• To move a casualty safely onto a stretcher

• To check for hidden wounds on the casualty’s back.

 

Safety

• Ensure the casualty’s head is immobilised at all times.

 

Immobilise the casualty’s head using either of the grips shown in the pictures -

 

 

 

 

 

If there is a significant head injury present, remove or loosen the collar once the casualty has been moved onto the stretcher -

 

 

The first person places one hand under the casualty’s shoulder

and the other hand on their hip. A second person places one hand

on the casualty’s ribcage and the other hand under their knee to create a cross-brace.  (Photo also shows the position of the hand under the casualty’s knee.)

 

 

Gently roll the casualty towards you as shown, on the command of the person holding the head.  Ensure the casualty’s head is immobilised at all times.

 

 

Move the stretcher of choice in behind the casualty and roll the casualty gently into the stretcher.
