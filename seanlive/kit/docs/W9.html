Gauze and Triangular bandage

 

Gauze can be used either to hold a non adherent dressing in place on a wound and is also the ideal bandage for packing deep holes in major wounds For use when packing, the gauze is unrolled and is pushed firmly into the wound area until it is completely packed and the bleed controlled More than one gauze dressing may be required to pack a large wound.

 

The triangular bandage can be used for slings, but frac straps are a much better option for immobilisation where possible. A triangular bandage however can be used for cooling facial burns. Soaked in cold water, and with some holes for the eyes, nose and mouth cut out. It can then be laid onto the patients face and cooling continued. A wet triangular bandage can also be draped over burn bags and cling film to assist in cooling of burns.
